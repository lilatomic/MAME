External Relations

The McGill Engineering Student Centre (MESC) offers many services to undergraduate students, integrating the Student Affairs Office (SAO), academic advising and peer tutoring.MESC administers students’ records and is an important resource for policies and procedures related to student affairs. This office also helps in advising and assisting students in program planning and in the proper selection of courses, so that all students follow programs that coincide with their talents, interests and goals. Visit their website for more information!

The Engineering Career Centre is also an integral part of the McGill Engineering Student Centre and provides students with opportunities to gain career-related experience through internships (Engineering Internship Program); connect with employers; learn about various career paths; develop their job search skills; and more. Visit ECC’s website and facebook page for more information !

The McGill Institute for Aerospace Engineering (MIAE) is an amazing resource for students interested in the aerospace industry. It an incredible environment for those who wish to expand their opportunities in the field, all while developing their related knowledge. Students who are accepted into the Institute are provided with resources and internship opportunities at Pratt & Whitney, Bombardier, Siemens, Bell Helicopter and more! They are also and informed of events such as plant visits to allow the pursuit of their interests. MIAE creates a mutually beneficial setting, providing students with unique and valuable experiences while introducing the industry to some of Canada’s brightest engineering students. For more information, check out their website!

The McGill Career Planning Service (CaPS) assists students in their career development and search for permanent, part-time, and summer jobs, as well as internships, by providing workshops, individual advising, a comprehensive job posting service, and an extensive Career Resource Centre. For a list of all events, log-in to myFuture and click on the Events tab.

Speaking of Events, TechFair is one the biggest networking events in engineering. TechFair is a collaborative effort combining the resources and expertise of the Engineering Career Centre (ECC), the Engineering Student Society (EUS) and the Computer Science Student Society (CSUS). Created in the year 2000, the TechFair intends to connect McGill students in Engineering, Computer Science and Physical Sciences with the top employers in the field for the purpose of exploring career and/or internship opportunities. For more information on TechFair, visit their website or their facebook page.

How does MAME Distribute its funds? MAME uses its funds to subsidize different events so we can offer as much for as many students as possible.
Where does MAME get funding for all these awesome things? MAME gets funding for its different activities from a variety of sources:
  • Academic/Professional
    • MECHMadness, Industry Dinner, Academic Form, CAD|Madness, Conferences, Workshops (Mechatronics, VBA etc.), Tutorials, Networking Events, Lunch & Learns, Mech Talks, LinkedIn Headshots, Lab Tours etc.
  • Social
    • Apartment Crawls, Holiday Dinner, End of Year Banquet, E-Week, Mech Sports Teams, Exam Snacks
  • Other
    • Clothing sale, Printing Charges, Elections, Year Specific Events
  • Student Fees
  • Funding from the EUS, Department of Mechanical Engineering and McGill Engineering Student Centre
  • External Sponsorship
  • Ticket Sales
  • Revenue Generating events such as Blues Pub or Samosa Sales
